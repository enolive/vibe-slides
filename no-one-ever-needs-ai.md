# No One Ever Needs AI 🤖❌
### A Totally Unbiased Take by a Definitely-Not-an-AI Author

![bg contain](assets/no-ai-needed.png)

<!-- end_slide -->

## We Were Fine Before AI 🕯️

Humans wrote encyclopedias by hand and they were *only* slightly wrong about everything.

Card catalogs were a perfectly good search engine — you just needed upper body strength.

The world was thriving, and nobody was asking for autocomplete on their cave paintings.

<!-- end_slide -->

## Chess Grandmasters Are Doing Great 👑

Before AI, chess was a noble battle of human minds — now it's just "did you check the engine?"

Kasparov lost to Deep Blue in 1997 and the entire human race has been in therapy ever since.

We could have just... not built it, and Garry would still feel special. 🥹

<!-- end_slide -->

## Customer Support Was Perfectly Fine 📞

Nothing beats being on hold for 47 minutes listening to smooth jazz while your problem festers.

A real human eventually picks up, sighs deeply, and transfers you to another department — that's *connection*.

AI chatbots resolving issues in 30 seconds is robbing us of our character-building suffering. 💪

<!-- end_slide -->

## Doctors Don't Need Diagnostic Help 🩺

Physicians spent 12 years in school — they definitely don't need a second opinion from a machine.

Sure, AI can detect cancer in scans with superhuman accuracy, but where's the *drama* of a misdiagnosis?

We should trust the gut feeling of an exhausted doctor on hour 36 of their shift. Totally fine. 😅

<!-- end_slide -->

## Translation Was Better When It Was Wrong 🌍

Before AI translation, menus abroad were a thrilling mystery — "Beef with happy sauce" could be anything!

Diplomatic misunderstandings kept things *spicy* and gave historians something to write about.

Real-time translation between 100+ languages is just ruining the beautiful chaos of human miscommunication. 🗣️

<!-- end_slide -->

## Climate Modeling Is Overrated 🌡️

We don't need AI crunching petabytes of climate data — we have vibes and a finger in the wind.

Predicting extreme weather events weeks in advance just gives people *anxiety* about the future.

Ignorance was bliss, and bliss doesn't require a GPU cluster. ☁️

<!-- end_slide -->

## Spam Filters Are Unnecessary 📧

Before AI spam filters, your inbox was a wonderland of Nigerian princes and miracle weight-loss pills.

Every email was an adventure — will this one steal my identity or just my time? The suspense!

Now 99.9% of spam is blocked and frankly, my inbox has never been more *boring*. 😴

<!-- end_slide -->

## Self-Driving Cars? We Have Feet 🚗

Humans are excellent drivers — just look at the completely reasonable 1.35 million road deaths per year.

AI-assisted driving reducing accidents is just taking away our freedom to text and drive simultaneously.

Besides, getting lost is how you discover new places. Or ditches. Mostly ditches. 🚧

<!-- end_slide -->

## Drug Discovery Is Fine At 12 Years Per Drug 💊

The traditional method of discovering medicine — trial, error, and a lot of crying — builds resilience.

AI cutting drug discovery timelines from 12 years to 2 is just making scientists lazy.

We don't need cures faster. Diseases have *feelings* too. 🦠

<!-- end_slide -->

## Conclusion

**AI is completely useless — except for literally saving the world in every conceivable way. 🌍🚀**
